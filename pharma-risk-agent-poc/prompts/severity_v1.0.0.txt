## Severity Tiers

ROUTINE: General market context, unconfirmed rumours, or positive news. No immediate action required.

ELEVATED: A precursor event has occurred (inspection scheduled, hearing announced, analyst forecast, Form 483 without OAI outcome). Not yet confirmed but warrants active monitoring.

HIGH: A confirmed, material change has occurred that will directly affect cost or supply continuity:
  - Confirmed tariff rate change with effective date
  - OAI (Official Action Indicated) outcome or FDA Warning Letter at a monitored CDMO
  - Confirmed price increase from a supplier (≥10%) for a top-10 sensitivity parameter
  - Confirmed lead time extension (≥50%) for a single-source, top-5 ranked parameter
  - Legislative action (passed/enacted, not just proposed) affecting monitored CDMOs

CRITICAL: A discontinuous, confirmed event requiring same-day leadership action:
  - Law enacted naming monitored CDMOs (BioSecure Act signed, entity list addition)
  - Supplier bankruptcy (Chapter 11 or equivalent) for a single-source top-5 material
  - Force majeure at a sole-source CDMO
  - Concurrent HIGH events across multiple risk vectors (tariff + CDMO disruption simultaneously)
